Privacy Policy for Pattina
Effective Date: May 5, 2026
Welcome to Pattina (internally referred to as Trailmark). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring your data is handled securely, in compliance with global standards including the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and share your information when you use our mobile application.
1. Information We Collect and Device Permissions
We collect information and request specific device permissions to provide and improve our services:
Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your email address, display name, and (optionally) your profile photo using Firebase Authentication. If you sign in using Google Sign-In or Apple Sign-In, those providers share your name and email address with us in accordance with their own privacy policies.
Camera Access: We request explicit access to your device's camera to allow you to take photos (referred to in the app as "captures") directly within Pattina.
Photo Library Access: We request access to your photo library so that you can import existing photos as captures. We only access photos you explicitly select.
Location Data (Foreground): We collect precise GPS location data to map your captures, place them on the map, provide local weather context, and power location-based features such as nearby exhibitions and pinned places.
Location Data (Background): If you choose to track an active hike, walk, or visit, we request background location access. This allows the app to continue tracking your route accurately even when the app is minimized or your phone screen is locked. Background location is only used while you have an active visit in progress and ceases when the visit ends.
Media and EXIF Metadata: We collect the photos you take or upload. If you import an existing image, we extract any GPS coordinates embedded in the image's EXIF metadata to accurately place the capture on your map. We do not extract or store EXIF data beyond location and timestamp.
App Usage & Preferences: We store your app preferences, such as selected theme, units of measurement, your chosen nature or art interests, and your language settings.
2. How We Use Your Information
Your data is used to power the core features of Pattina:
AI Identification: We send your captured images, along with coarse location data (country, region, city), to our AI provider (Anthropic) to identify flora, fauna, cultural artifacts, and other subjects in your photos.
Journal Generation: We use your tracked visit data (durations, locations visited, captures taken) and capture content to generate AI-written journal entries, briefs, and weekly insights.
Community Heatmap (Opt-In): If you choose to opt-in to public heatmap contributions, we process your location into aggregated, precision-7 geohashes. A precision-7 geohash represents a grid cell of approximately 150 metres by 150 metres, which obscures your exact pinpoint coordinates while still allowing aggregated visualisation of community exploration hotspots.
Public Sharing (Opt-In): If you choose to share a capture, collection, or visit externally (for example, via a shareable link), we generate a public URL that allows the recipient to view that specific content. You control which content is shared and can revoke shared links at any time.
Analytics and Insights: We analyse your recent captures and visit data to generate personalised weekly insights, monthly reports, and similarity recommendations within the app.
3. Third-Party Service Providers and International Transfers
To operate Pattina, we share specific data with trusted third-party processors. Because we use global infrastructure, your data may be transferred to and processed in the United States and other jurisdictions. We ensure these transfers are protected by appropriate legal safeguards, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where applicable.
Google Firebase (US): Used for database hosting (Firestore), cloud storage, authentication, and serverless backend functions. Receives your account data, captures, visits, and journal content.
Anthropic (US): We send your captured images and contextual location data (coarse only) to Anthropic's Claude AI models for subject identification and journal writing. Anthropic does not retain customer data for training purposes under our usage agreement.
Mapbox (US): We share your coordinates with Mapbox for map rendering, routing, and reverse geocoding (converting coordinates into place names).
RevenueCat (US): Used to validate purchase receipts and manage in-app subscription entitlements. Receives anonymous user identifiers and transaction data from Apple or Google.
OpenWeather API: We send your approximate location to OpenWeather to fetch local 5-day forecasts.
iNaturalist API: We query iNaturalist to verify taxonomy, scientific names, and spatial plausibility of identified species. No personally identifying information is sent.
Apple Sign-In and Google Sign-In: If you use these to authenticate, your name and email are shared with us by Apple or Google in accordance with their respective privacy policies.
4. Your Rights Under UK GDPR and GDPR
You have the following rights regarding your personal data:
Right of Access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Right to Rectification: You can correct inaccurate data through the app's settings or by contacting us.
Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You can delete your account directly within the app. All associated user data — including stored images, route histories, captures, collections, and journal entries — is permanently and completely deleted from our servers within 30 days of your deletion request.
Right to Data Portability: You can request an export of your captures, visits, and journal data in a machine-readable format.
Right to Restriction of Processing: You can request that we limit how we process your data in specific circumstances.
Right to Object: You can object to certain types of processing, including the use of your data for the community heatmap (which is opt-in only and can be turned off at any time in settings).
Right to Lodge a Complaint: UK residents have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk. EU residents have the right to complain to their local data protection authority.
To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address provided in Section 8.
5. Data Retention
We retain your personal data, captures, and journey histories only for as long as your account is active. If you choose to delete your account, your data is scheduled for permanent deletion. All associated user data, including stored images, route histories, captures, and AI-generated journal content, is completely and permanently deleted from our servers within 30 days of your request.
Anonymised, aggregated heatmap contributions (geohashed location signals) may be retained beyond account deletion, as they cannot be linked back to your identity.
Backup systems may retain residual copies for up to 90 days due to standard disaster-recovery practices, after which they are also overwritten or deleted.
6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies
Pattina is a mobile application and does not use traditional web cookies. However, we use local device storage and third-party Software Development Kits (SDKs) — such as Firebase, RevenueCat, and Mapbox — to maintain your login session, save your preferences, validate subscriptions, and ensure the app functions correctly. These SDKs may store identifiers locally on your device.
7. Children's Privacy
Pattina is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from users under the age of 16. If we become aware that a child under 16 has provided us with personal information without verifiable parental consent, we will take immediate steps to delete such information from our servers. If you believe a child has provided us with their personal data, please contact us using the details below.
8. Changes to This Policy
